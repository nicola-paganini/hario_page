HARIO

The iconic Hario V60, a popular choice among beginners and experienced baristas for it's easy to use design and wide choices of material construction.

It features a unique spiral design and a centre large hole to allows for fast and even coffee extraction for busy mornings.

HISTORY

Established in 1921, Hario started out as a manufacturer for scientific glassware and beakers. It wasn't until 1980 that Hario would innovate the very first connical shape dripper that challenged the prevaling use of traditional cloth drippers.

By 2005, the Hario V60 dripper started to gain popular momentum along with the "third-wave coffee culture", emphasising a more experimental and customised coffee drinking experience.

The dripper's unique spiral rib pattern creates air passages that allows more precise control over the flow rate of water, resulting in a clean and smoother of cup of coffee in contrast to traditional methods.

Additionally, The V60 dripper popularised the art of Japanese coffeee brewing to western countries, which mainly relied on heavy espresso machines, moka pots, and french presses as their default coffee brewing method.

RECIPE

Watch this step-by-step tutorial from reowned coffee expert, James Hoffman!

Coffee: 15g
Water: 250g (88-90â„ƒ)

  1. Rinse paper in V60 and pre heat it.
  2. Pour in coffee, make a well with your finger in the coffee bed.
  3. Start timer and gently pour 45g of water to bloom.
  4. Swirl and wait for 30-45 secs.
  5. During the next 30 seconds, slowly pour 120g of water.
  6. Finish pouring 250g of water before the timer hits 1 min 45 secs.
  7. Gently stir with a spoon in a circular motion once or twice.
  8. Take the dripper off from the server and enjoy!
